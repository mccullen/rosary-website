{{ whatIsTheRosary.text }}

The Rosary is a prayer of meditation on the mysteries of the Gospel

The Rosary is a blessed blending of mental and vocal prayer by which we honour and learn to imitate the mysteries and the virtues of the life, death, passion and glory of Jesus and Mary.

St. Louis-Marie de Montfort, The Admirable Secret of the Rosary, First Rose

{{ kindsOfPrayer.text }}

The Rosary consists of two kinds of prayer: vocal prayer and mental prayer.

{{ vocalPrayers.text }}

The vocal prayers of the Rosary consists in saying twenty decades of the Hail Mary, each headed by an Our Father and followed by a Glory Be to the Father and the optional Fatima prayer.

See the first Rose of The Admirable Secret of the Rosary

{{ mentalPrayers.text }}

The mental prayers of the Rosary consists in meditating upon and contemplating the twenty mysteries of the chief mysteries of the life, ministry, death, and glory of Jesus Christ and His Blessed Mother at the same time the vocal prayers are being recited.

See the first Rose of The Admirable Secret of the Rosary

{{ fruitOfTheMysteries.text }}

The fruits of the Mysteries of the Rosary are the principle virtues which Jesus and Mary practiced and we seek to imitate.

See the first Rose of The Admirable Secret of the Rosary

{{ seriesOfTheRosary.text }}

The Twenty Mysteries of the Rosary are grouped into four series of five decades each:

  1. Joyful Mysteries: Mysteries of Jesus's early life and the incarnation
  2. Mysteries of Light: Mysteries of Jesus's later life and ministry
  3. Sorrowful Mysteries: Mysteries of Jesus's passion and death
  4. Glorious Mysteries: Mysteries of Jesus's resurrection and glory

{{ originOfTheRosary.text }}

The origin of the Rosary is the Prayer of Christ and the Angelic Salutation.

See the second Rose of The Admirable Secret of the Rosary

{{ evolutionOfTheRosary.text }}

1214: The Church received the Rosary in its present form after St. Dominic received it from the Blessed Virgin to convert the Albigensians (who no longer exist) and other sinners. (St. Louis-Marie de Montfort, The Admirable Secret of the Rosary, Second Rose)

At this time, it was called the Psalter of Jesus and Mary because it had the same number of Angelic Salutations as there are psalms in the Book of the Psalms of David (150). Since simple and uneducated people could not say David’s Psalter, they would say the Psalter of Jesus and Mary instead (St. Louis-Marie de Montfort, The Admirable Secret of the Rosary , Sixth Rose).

1460: The Rosary, having lost its first fervor after a century, is restored by Blessed Alan de la Roche’s preaching after Our Lord spoke to him in the Sacred Host and reproached him for not preaching the Rosary despite his learning and understanding (St. Louis-Marie de Montfort, The Admirable Secret of the Rosary, Fourth Rose).

2002: St. Pope John Paul II introduced the “Mysteries of Light” (the Luminous Mysteries) as an optional series of mysteries in his Apostolic Letter on the Rosary, Rosarium Virginis Mariae, and provides guidance on how to better pray the Rosary.
